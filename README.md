You need specific details to connect to a remote server. Gather this information from your hosting provider or network administrator:

- Server address or host name.
- Port number.
- Username.
- Password.

Open FlashFXP-2026 from your desktop icon. Select Quick Connect from the menu. Enter your server details into the fields. Click Connect to start your session. The main window shows your local files on the left and remote files on the right.

## 📤 Transfer files

Move files between two locations using the interface. Select the files you want to send on the left side of the screen. Right-click the selected files and choose Transfer. You can also drag files from one side to the other. The status bar at the bottom shows the progress of each file.

## 🔐 Security settings

FlashFXP-2026 uses encryption to protect your data. Use SFTP whenever the server supports it. You can check your connection settings in the site manager. Ensure the encryption option reads "Require SFTP" to keep your transfer private.

## 💡 Best practices for transfers

Use the queue system for large sets of files. This keeps your connections stable. Verify your file sizes after a transfer if you move important data. Set your connection timeout to 60 seconds if your server experiences high traffic. This helps maintain the connection without manual intervention.

## 📊 Performance tips

Close other applications that use your internet connection during large transfers. This leaves more bandwidth for your file transfers. Keep your local machine free of viruses to ensure smooth operations. Check your firewall settings if you cannot connect to a server. You may need to grant permission to FlashFXP-2026 in the Windows Security settings.
